AFI About

With funding and technical assistance from Center for Disease Control and Protection (CDC), ICAP in collaboration with Ministry of Health (MOH) Kenya is implementing an Integrated Health Facility-Based Surveillance (IFBS) project in 10 health facilities in Kenya. The overall objective of the surveillance project is to improve health security in Kenya by contributing to early detection, reporting and response to outbreaks of emerging and endemic priority infectious diseases and conditions. This dashboard shows surveillance data collected in these health facilities since project launch in 2017. Currently, the project is active in 10/13 health facilities.

Dashboard Pages

1.About - contains information on the project and dashboard pages.

2.Overview - displays summary data including snapshot visuals from each page. Here, you will find number of patients screened, eligible, enrolled, sampled and results of Rapid Diagnostic Testing (RDT) and Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) tests.

3.Syndromic cases - shows distribution of cases including Severe Acute Respiratory Illness (SARI), Undifferentiated Fever (UF) and Diarrhea Fever (DF) over time and by health facility.

4.Laboratory results - visualizes laboratory results by health facility over time.

5.Diagnosis and outcome - shows patient diagnosis as documented in the file and the outcome at the time the patient was discharged from the hospital.
