SARI/ILI : About

Currently, influenza surveillance is ongoing at Kenyatta National Hospital(KNH), Coast General Teaching and Referral Hospital (CGTRH), Kakamega County Referral Hospital (CRH), Nakuru Country Referral Hospital(CRH), Nyeri Country Referral Hospital (NCRH), Marsabit Country Referral Hospital (CRH), Siaya Country Referral Hospital (SCRH), in the refugee camps in Kakuma and Daadab. The focus is to enroll both influenza-like illness (ILI) and severe acute respiratory illness (SARI).

Patients presenting with respiratory symptoms at outpatient clinics (ILI) and patients admitted with respiratory illness (SARI) in the inpatient wards are approached to be part of the surveillance. They are consented to allow collection of clinical and epidemiological data as well as clinical specimens for laboratory testing for influenza, SARS-CoV-2 and possibly other respiratory pathogens.

The program is currently implemented through Henry Jackson Foundation (HJF) and Kenya Medical Research Institute (KEMRI) to support

Ministry of Health (MOH) in monitoring of this surveillance with financial support from US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

This dashboard will be used to display the data collected from all the sites in chart format to allow faster synthesis of the data and inform a timely response as appropriate.
